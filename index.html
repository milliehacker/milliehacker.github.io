I'm a 1st-year PhD student in Linguistics at Michigan State University and an RA for the MI Diaries corpus project. I study phonological variation, with a current focus on intra-speaker variation and how/when/why narrative speech (story-telling) conditions the emergence of a speaker's native vernacular features. Fundamentally, I want to understand how the speaker's core phonological grammar interfaces with their social-functional considerations. My other main interest is in Optimality Theory, in which I've done some work on the relationship between reduction and deletion in Hixkaryana. Side interests include: hiatus resolution in Ojibwe, Comparative Markedness, language and gender, and the phonological representation of acronyms.
