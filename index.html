I'm a 1st-year linguistics PhD student at Michigan State University and an RA for the MI Diaries corpus project. I study phonological variation, and am especially interested in the factors that motivate intra-speaker variation (code-switching, style, register, etc.) I want to understand how the core phonological grammar interfaces with the speaker's social-functional considerations. To that end, my other main interest is in Optimality Theory and I choose to view the theory's preoccupation with groundedness as its greatest asset.

About me. I was born in Oakland, California, and attended UC Santa Cruz to study Linguistics and Environmental Studies. Outside my academic life, I like all kinds of visual arts, as well as running, dancing, hiking, biking (basically anything where I get to move my legs), baking, and exchanging letters with friends. I'm a proud member of the Spartan Housing Cooperative and currently serve on its board of directors. Ask me about co-ops if you're interested in healing mass loneliness and advancing a more conscious and sustainable way of living.
