I'm a 1st-year PhD student in Linguistics at Michigan State University and an RA for the MI Diaries corpus project. I study phonological variation, with a current focus on intra-speaker variation (code-switching, style, register, etc.) Fundamentally, I want to understand how the core phonological grammar interfaces with a speaker's social-functional considerations. To that end, my other main interest is in Optimality Theory, and I choose to view the theory's basis in groundedness (substance, markedness, function) as its greatest asset.
